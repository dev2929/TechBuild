Processor

The Brain of Your PC The Central Processing Unit (CPU) executes every command and handles the logic for your operating system and applications. For gamers, a higher clock speed ensures smooth frame rates; for creators, more cores allow for faster video rendering and multitasking.

graphic card

The Visual Powerhouse The Graphics Processing Unit (GPU) is the most critical component for gaming and 3D work. It calculates and renders every pixel you see on the screen. Modern cards feature "Ray Tracing" for realistic lighting and "DLSS/FSR" for AI-powered performance boosts.

motherboard

The Nervous System The Motherboard acts as the hub that connects all your components together. It determines how many RAM sticks you can use, how many SSDs you can plug in, and whether you can overclock your CPU. It also houses your rear I/O ports like USB and Ethernet.

memory

The Workspace Random Access Memory (RAM) is your computer's short-term memory. It stores data that your CPU needs to access instantly. More RAM allows you to keep more Chrome tabs open, run heavier games, and edit larger files without your system slowing down.

storage

The Vault This is where your files, games, and OS live permanently. NVMe M.2 SSDs are the gold standard today, offering speeds up to $50\times$ faster than traditional Hard Drives (HDD). Use an SSD for your Windows drive and an HDD if you need massive, cheap storage for movies or photos.

power supply

The Heartbeat The PSU converts wall electricity into the specific voltages your PC components need. A high-quality PSU protects your expensive hardware from power surges and ensures system stability under heavy gaming loads.
